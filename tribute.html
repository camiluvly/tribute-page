Anne Spencer

1882-1975

American Author and Poet



Anne Spencer's Life

  • 1882 - Born on a farm in Henry County, Virginia

  • 1893 - Brought to Lynchburg, Virginia to attend the Virginia Theological Seminary and College

  • 1899 - Graduates from the Virginia Theological Seminary and College, and delivers the valedictory address

  • 1901 - Anne and Edward Spencer marry

  • 1903 - Moved a the Queen Anne-style home Edward designed and built for them

  • 1910-1912 - Taught at Virginia Seminary

  • 1913 - Anne, Edward, and other community members founded the Lynchburg Chapter of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP)

  • 1920 - First poem is published at the age of 40, Before the Feast at Shushan

  • 1924 - Anne brought a copy of her publications to her interview for a position as a librarian. With this, she was able to persuade the Lynchburg school board to let her lead the first library open to the city"s Black citizens. She started the library in the segregated Dunbar High school with a loan of her own books

  • 1942 - Retired from Dunbar High School

  • 1975 - Died at the age of 93
